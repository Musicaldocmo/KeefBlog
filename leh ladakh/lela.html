Discover Leh & Ladakh

Explore the serene beauty, adventure, and rich culture of the Leh & Ladakh region. From scenic lakes and ancient monasteries to thrilling adventures, experience everything this Himalayan paradise has to offer.

Pangong Tso Lake

Pangong Tso Lake is a high-altitude lake at 4,350 meters, famous for its vibrant blue color that changes with the sunlight. Stretching from India into Tibet, the lake offers breathtaking views, ideal for photography and sightseeing.

Hall of Fame Museum

This museum, established by the Indian Army, commemorates the bravery of Indian soldiers. It showcases artifacts, biographies, and memorials, offering a deep dive into the region’s military history and Ladakhi culture.

Tso Moriri Lake

Located at 4,595 meters, Tso Moriri Lake is one of Ladakh’s most beautiful lakes, surrounded by mountains and a serene atmosphere. This lesser-visited lake is a haven for birdwatchers and provides an escape into Ladakh’s tranquil wilderness.

River Rafting on Zanskar River

Embark on an exhilarating river rafting adventure on the Zanskar River, where the icy waters cut through steep gorges. This thrilling experience is a must for adrenaline seekers visiting Ladakh.

Shanti Stupa

Perched on a hilltop, Shanti Stupa offers panoramic views of Leh and the surrounding mountains. Built to promote world peace, the stupa is a popular spot for witnessing sunrise and sunset over the city.

Bactrian Camel Ride in Nubra Valley

Experience the unique Bactrian camel ride on the sand dunes of Nubra Valley. These double-humped camels, a legacy of the Silk Route, provide a distinct desert experience amid Ladakh’s cold desert landscape.

Nubra Valley

Nubra Valley, known for its scenic beauty, sand dunes, and monasteries, is a cultural and natural hotspot in Ladakh. The valley is accessible through the Khardung La Pass and offers a mix of landscapes, from lush greenery to arid deserts.

Khardung La Pass

Khardung La, one of the highest motorable passes in the world at 5,359 meters, offers breathtaking views of the Himalayas. It serves as the gateway to Nubra Valley and is popular among motorcyclists and adventure enthusiasts.

Zanskar Valley

Zanskar Valley is known for its stunning landscapes and opportunities for trekking, camping, and river rafting. It’s a remote paradise, offering an escape into Ladakh’s rugged wilderness,One of the most challenging treks in India, Chadar Trek involves trekking over the frozen Zanskar River. It offers a unique experience of walking on ice, surrounded by towering cliffs and untouched landscapes is also done Here.

Stok Palace

Stok Palace, the residence of the Ladakhi royal family, is a blend of traditional architecture and modern art. The palace museum houses artifacts and provides insight into Ladakhi culture and heritage.

Kargil

Kargil, famous for the 1999 conflict, holds historical and strategic importance. It is a base for exploring nearby attractions and a key point along the Srinagar-Leh highway.

Leh Palace

Constructed in the 17th century, Leh Palace is a nine-story structure overlooking Leh town. It offers panoramic views and a glimpse into Ladakh’s royal heritage.

Hemis National Park

Home to the elusive snow leopard, Hemis National Park is a must-visit for wildlife enthusiasts. It also offers trekking trails and is home to rare bird species, making it a biodiversity hotspot.

Other Notable Attractions

  • Magnetic Hill: An optical illusion that makes it appear vehicles roll uphill.
  • Lamayuru Monastery: Known as the oldest monastery in Ladakh with scenic views.
  • Drass Valley: One of the coldest inhabited places, famous for trekking and scenic beauty.
  • Sham Valley: Known as the “Apricot Valley,” ideal for short treks and village tours.
